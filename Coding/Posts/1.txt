Video by @carltonward | On January 17 the Florida governor and cabinet approved funding for the permanent conservation of 15,390 acres (6,200 ha) across four properties within or adjacent to a contiguous tract known as the Florida Wildlife Corridor.The property in this video will protect nearly a mile (1.5 km) of shoreline along the spring-fed Rainbow River through a conservation easement.The other corridor-connected properties approved for protection include Telogia Creek, Osceola Pine Savannas, and Triple Diamond Ranch.
